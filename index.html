Hello! I'm Medhansh Bhagchandani, a Chemical Engineering student at Cornell University (Class of 2029). I care deeply about engineering solutions that address real-world challenges in energy and sustainability.

Before Cornell, I served two years as a Group Leader with the Singapore Police Force, safeguarding millions of daily commuters, training officer cohorts, and leading operations at national events — an experience that shaped my approach to high-stakes leadership.

Across research and project roles — at the UNC Water Institute, University of Houston, CISTAR, and Cornell's Engineers Without Borders international project team — I've worked on everything from systematic reviews of global water quality to catalytic dry reforming of methane and sustainable engineering in underserved communities. Outside the lab, you'll find me on long runs, reading about policy, or exploring new cities.
